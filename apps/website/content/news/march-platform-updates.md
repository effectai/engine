March has been a strong month for Effect. We shipped several worker-facing improvements, expanded our task template library, launched a new public dataset, and made critical progress on infrastructure that will enable smoother scaling. Here's what landed.

## Worker Experience Gets Better

We added a **TaskPosterStatus component** that gives workers live visibility into task availability across the platform. This removes the guesswork: contributors can now see which tasks are active, queued, or coming soon. Real-time task status helps workers plan their time and reduces friction in the discovery process.

This is paired with a new **scheduling system** behind the scenes that allows task requesters to queue tasks for specific times. Workers benefit immediately: they see the upcoming pipeline and can prepare for high-value work. Task requesters get better control over when their work goes live.

Together, these changes signal a shared goal: make the platform predictable for everyone involved.

---

## New Templates and Datasets

Our task template library now includes a **new image annotation tool with bounding boxes**: a powerful addition for computer vision datasets and quality control workflows. Workers draw precise bounding boxes around objects in images, label them, and the system captures both the spatial data and the labels. The tool integrates seamlessly with our existing validation architecture.

Here's our complete template roster:

- **Sentence Generation**: Free-form text creation
- **Sentence Recording**: Audio capture with quality validation
- **Image Classification and Metadata Creation**: Multi-label classification
- **Image Annotation with Bounding Boxes** *(new)*: Precise spatial annotation
- **Music Lyric Transcription**: Audio to text
- **Full Song Lyric Transcription Validation**: Multi-stage verification

All templates include built-in validation steps to ensure data quality.

We also introduced the **Novela dataset**, a significant addition that expands the range of work available to contributors. Novela is built around creating recordings of human-read audio paired with sentences, helping train text-to-speech models and LLMs on natural human conversation speech. This work is foundational for building AI systems that sound and respond like real people. Contributors working on Novela are directly supporting the next generation of conversational AI.

## Website Gets a Dedicated Blog Space

Our news and updates are growing. For a while, we featured blog posts in a carousel on our homepage, but that format wasn't giving them the space and visibility they deserved. We've now built a **dedicated blogs page** for the Effect AI website with a featured section highlighting the latest post. This makes it easier for the community to discover, read, and follow platform updates, learnings, and announcements. It also signals to the outside world that we're invested in sharing what we're building and learning along the way.

---

## State Syncing (Coming Soon)

Behind the scenes, we're laying groundwork for seamless multi-device support. Our upcoming **state syncing feature** will eliminate the fragmentation that happens when workers use different devices or browsers. Right now, switching from a phone to a desktop means starting fresh as a different worker account. With state syncing, your worker capabilities, task progress, rewards, qualifications, and all other metrics will sync automatically across platforms. Start a task on your phone during a commute, pick it up on your laptop at home, and resume on a tablet later, all without losing context or progress.

This is a foundational piece for a truly permissionless network: contributors should be able to pick up work from anywhere, on any device, without friction. The feature is currently in internal testing and expected to deploy within Q2.
